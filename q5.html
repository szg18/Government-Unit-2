Grand Jury v Petit Jury



Grand Jury - Examines evidence to see if there is sufficient ground for prosecution. Will issue an indictment if enough evidence to send to trial. Petit Jury - In criminal cases, they need an unanimous decision. In civil cases, only require a â…” of the jury.


6th & 7th Amendment



6th Amendment - The right to a jury in a criminal case. 7th Amendment - The right to a jury in a civil case.


4th and 5th Amendment



4th Amendment - Guards against unreasonable searches and seizures 5th Amendment - The right to due process and no double jeopardy


Civil and criminal cases



Civil Justice System: Parties = Plaintiff - the victim Defendant - the perpetrator and any other person or business who contributed to the abuse, Process = Average time to resolve is 1.5 years excluding any appeals Result = Financial recovery for the plaintiff Criminal Justice System: Parties = Plaintiff - Commonwealth/State Defendant - perpetrator Process = Average time to resolve is 1 year excluding any appeals Result = Criminal punishment for the defendant


Texas v. Johnson



Texas v. Johnson, 491 U.S. 397, was a decision by the Supreme Court of the United States that invalidated prohibitions on desecrating the American flag enforced in 48 of the 50 states
